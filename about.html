I'm a full-stack web developer with a background in art and design. Like many Millenials, I grew up with unfettered access to the internet. I ran free on the web - discovering a new creative outlet and beginning a lifelong passion for making things. Customizing Livejournal themes with HTML turned into studying Fiber and Material Studies at the School of the Art Institute.

At art school, a Digital Jacquard Weaving professor taught the relationship between woven textiles and programming. Ada Lovelace, the first computer programmer, took inspiration from the Jacquard loom to develop a theoretical all-purpose computer with Charles Babbage. Ada Lovelace became a hero of mine. She pursued her interest in mathmetics even though it was unheard of for a woman of her time. She had insight and realized that Babbage's machine was capable of much more than its original purpose of numerical calculations. I made a promise to myself to pursue programming later in life.

After a decade working in textiles, retail and e-commerce, I now help companies build the web. When I'm not in front of a screen, you can find me hiking upstate with my Carolina Dog, Dexter, dancing to Megan thee Stallion, cooking Korean food, or reading Jane Jacobs.
